About

A founder story organized around early code, infrastructure, communications, and AI systems.

Through-line

A lifelong builder, not a static bio.

Ray Bolouri’s work is framed by a long habit of building systems that make communication and information more useful. The story begins with early code and continues through infrastructure, messaging, voice, and agentic AI.

The important pattern is not a list of titles. It is the repeated move from technical possibility to practical use: what should the system help people do, and what should it leave out?

Exact historical dates and metrics should be added only after verification. Until then, the site uses era-based framing and selected-build language.

Values

Useful technology over technical theater.

Clarity

Make the purpose legible.

A system earns attention when people can understand what it helps them do.

Judgment

Use machines where they are strong.

Repetition, organization, and precision belong to machines. Context and judgment stay human.

Continuity

Connect eras without exaggeration.

The archive treats history as a set of signals, not as proof inflated by unsupported claims.
